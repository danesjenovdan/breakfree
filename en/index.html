On Wednesday, the Slovenian government approved the third anti-corona bill which is effective until the end of 2021 and cancels all environmental safeguards regarding large construction projects:

  1. It allows investors to build without a final building permit.
  2. It subordinates competent public institutions and puts them in the service of private investors.
  3. It opens the door to abuse, as it removes from proceedings, anyone who could draw attention to exploitations, by which it also:
  4. Abolishes the control of courts over the decisions of the Ministry of the Environment.

We therefore call on members of the Slovenian parliament to reject the provisions of the latestanti-corona bill because:

We want development. But not development that will destroy rivers, clearcut our forests, turn the best agricultural land into concrete and devastate our biodiversity.

We want investment. But not investments aimed only at the profits of investors, disregardingsustainable development of society and the economy.

We want jobs. But not the jobs promised before construction, which are never realized after the end of the project.

We want a Ministry of the Environment that will protect the environment and protect nature, as well as thoroughly assess the impact of potential investments on the environment ‚Äì not encourage construction investments at any cost.

We want strong safeguards against mistakes and corruption. Therefore, we want the possibility for suspicious decisions of the ministries to be judged in court as well.

We want professionalism. Therefore, we want experts from competent public institutions to have a say in the process of issuing building permits, and we want competent public institutions to have the right to a give a negative opinion.

We want public debate. We want the views of civil society and experts to be considered. We don't want measures that eliminate public opinion and are contrary to the Slovenian Constitution, EU environmental law and international conventions.

We want sustainable development. That is why we want to transform the economy and society in such a way as to preserve a healthy, living environment and protect our natural resources - for us and for future generations.

We want a healthy, secure and democratic future for ourselves and for all who will come after us.

We therefore urge you: Reject the controversial provisions of the third anti-corona bill.
